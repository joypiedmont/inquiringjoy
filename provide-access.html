"Librarians and governing bodies should maintain that parents — and only parents — have the right and the responsibility to restrict the access of their children — and only their children — to library resources." — Free Access to Libraries for Minors, ALA.org

Access to information is not just a right extended towards adults; children and young adults also have this right. Furthermore, school libraries should provide a rich and diverse collection of resources free from censorship. A school's administration and librarian should work together to ensure the students' rights to access information is protected.

For more on the related issue of censorship in libraries, visit the censorship page.
