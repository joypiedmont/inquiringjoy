Facebook, MySpace, twitter, delicious, flickr, formspring, blogs, etc., are changing the ways that people interact with information. Young adults are connecting with their peers, and others, through these sites, and their corresponding information experiences are unique. Mobile electronic devices like the Kindle, iPad, smartphones, and mp3 players are also influencing the ways in which young adults interact with information.

School library media specialists, need to be particularly aware of the various new media students use to engage with information. Being alert to how these online communities and electronic devices affect the information behavior of students is key to providing the best library experience.
